Health in South Sudan

The healthcare situation is critical, particularly for children. In 2015, UNICEF reported that a fifteen-year-old girl is likely to die during childbirth than going to school. In addition, more than 80% of South Sudanese have no access to toilets, the report added. The government faces an immense task in dealing with lack of qualified staff and healthcare facilities in order to meet the needs of its people. Thus, it becomes an additional pressure on the healthcare system (Ref. https://www.humanium.org/en/south-sudan). The situation calls for an integrated approach that links relief, recovery and sustainable development interventions to support affected families to recover from the shocks and enhance resilience.

Hope for Children In Needs

Hope for Children in Need (HCN) is a national, not for profit, non-governmental organization, founded in April 2011 as ‘Hope for Vulnerable Children’ and later rebranded after a meeting convened by Board of Directors through consultant’s advices and made resolution to name it as ‘Hope for Children in Need (HCN)’ in 2019; however, its logo remained unchanged. The organization is founded by group of South Sudanese with different educational background with an aim of contributing to fight poverty. HCN engages the expertise of Individual Humanitarians from Neighboring Countries like Uganda, Kenya, Sudan, Ethiopia, Democratic Republic of Congo and Central African Republic to meet its goals. It also accepts volunteers from oversea or different part of the world based on their expertise to come and volunteer in South Sudan. HCN works according to the international development and humanitarian principles.

Our Thematic Focus


Focus 1: Emergency Response and Disaster Risk Reduction: With increasing vulnerability of local communities to natural and man-made disasters, many disadvantaged groups, especially local farmers, women and children remains at risk to various shocks and hazards. We aim to contribute to the National Adaptive Program for Climate Change enrolled by the Ministry of Humanitarian Affairs and Disaster Management of the Government of South Sudan. Our overall Disaster Risk Reduction program address livelihood vulnerability through the following thematic focuses;

(i) Food Security and Livelihood in Emergency: We aim to improved rural livelihood strategies of the vulnerable communities through provision of integrated Food Security package that incorporates introduction of climate adaptive livelihood measures and capacity building of local communities towards predicted conditions of climate change. We aim to revitalize the economy through implementation of Market-Based Intervention. In other hands, it’s too generous to revitalize a country’s economy like South Sudan by an NGO; however, we are aiming to revitalize the living standard of individual low incomes families through provision of microfinance. We believe that it’s a vital solution to poverty reduction. Furthermore, our programme also include women literacy. This will enhance women to formally manage their own incomes.

(ii) Water, Hygiene and Sanitation in Emergency: About 65% of population across South Sudan still have inadequate access to clean drinking water with about 74% of the population have limited access to safe sanitation facilities (UNICEF, 2017). Due to inadequate WASH services, there is continue prevalence of WASH-related outbreaks such as cholera across the country, affecting mostly women and children. Despite the ongoing efforts of various local and international humanitarian actors, there are sufficient evidences to scale-up WASH coverage across communities in South Sudan. We aim to deliver integrated WASH response targeting vulnerable IDPs and host communities in the country.

Focus 2: Food Security and Livelihood; South Sudan still faces instability in agricultural sectors which is the back bone of the country’s economy and food security for the entire population. There are also inadequate supports to individuals and groups who have better ideas through innovative projects that provide alternatives to youths in boosting economic growth. HCN is therefore committed to support such ideas through programs such as household and economic recovery projects, entrepreneurial development projects with special focus on Micro-Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) and creative initiative interventions.

Focus 3: Education and Peace building; according to report from UNICEF, South Sudan continues to have the highest number of illiteracies with literacy rate of 27%. This is due to insufficient access to basic educational support. In addition, with the recent declaration of National Dialogue, there is need to incorporate community level peace building and reconciliation initiatives. We therefore aim to increase access to basic education services targeting mainly children as well as promoting girl child education and adult literacy. We also aim to initiate community level dialogue initiatives, national building and democracy as means of promoting co-existence of members of different communities across the country in line with the recently declared national dialogue, and increase the participation of youth and women in nation building.

Focus 4: Water, Sanitation and Hygiene; South Sudan still lacks clean drinking water despite the efforts made during the time of transitional government and after independence of South Sudan in July 2011. Currently, we majorly aim to operate in IDPs and hard to reach areas to drill and repair boreholes if opportunities allow.

Focus 5: Integrated Health; Health services in South Sudan remain one of the greater challenges to the entire population. We aim to introduce health intervention which include integrated health and nutrition to help the affected IDPs, returnees and the host communities. It has come to our notice that children and youth are suffering of deadly diseases which either have no treatment or no proper treatment to compact such diseases especially when people are forced to stay in places such as IDPs’ camps, refugee camps, or towns seen as safer place.
Geographical Focus: We aim to reach diversity of communities across South Sudan; however, we are also aware of the need to expand strategically and coherently with our operational capacity as well as available resources in order to maximize our impacts to the people we serve. The organization is legally registered to operate in all the ten states and other administrative areas across South Sudan. Due to lack of funding, HCN headquarter is now in Bor town of Jonglei state where some minimal activities are being carryout.

HCN commitments and current status: HCN 2021-2022 Humanitarian and Development strategy aim to achieve lasting impacts on the lives of disadvantaged women, men, boys and girls affected by humanitarian crisis as well as promote resilient communities that is/are able to withstand shocks and threats. While delivering
services, we strongly emphasis on adhering to humanitarian best practices and principles. We maintain close contacts with our beneficiaries through formal and informal accountability framework.

Main Objectives

1. To improve the lives of disaster affected families and mitigation of disaster risks through provision of integrated package of life-enhancing services –child protection, psychosocial support, Gender Based Violent, and Non-Food Items to promote bottom-up approach through provision of health and nutrition program to the communities we serve.

2. To contribute on enhancing the improvement of market accessibilities and development of Micro-Small Medium Enterprises (MSMEs), agricultural products, local food supplies through capacitating the entrepreneurs in South Sudan.

3. To promote inclusive Education support to the children with special focus on girls and children with special needs, and empower adults, women (small scale and micro finance), and provide support to youths in participatory approach and evidence-based in fighting HIV/AIDS.

4. To increase access to clean drinking water, hygiene and improvement of sanitation facilities for disadvantaged rural households across the country.

5. Other possible objectives include:

✓ To implement risk informed, gender responsive and inclusive development and disaster risk management programmes with a rights-based perspective
✓ To improve the socio-economic status of the poor, marginalized and vulnerable groups in rural areas of South Sudan
✓ To provide educational opportunities to children in need, and mobilize or provide scholarships to needy students to pursue their college studies
✓ To conduct awareness on public health, environment, agriculture, and disaster related program such as climate change
✓ To advocate and campaign at the local, Payams/County and National levels on the underlying causes of poverty and vulnerability, human rights violations, and key development issues
✓ To conduct applied research on relevant socio-economic to alleviate poverty, climate, disaster and environmental issues and utilize the learning within its integrated development and risk reduction programmes
